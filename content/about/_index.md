The Gwent MG Owners Club is a friendly, welcoming club primarily for MG enthusiasts across South East Wales. We are affiliated to the MG Owners' Club and bring together owners and fans of all MG models. Members come from all walks of life, but all share a common passion for classic cars. And we welcome those with other types of British classic car too. 

Whether you’re a seasoned classic car owner or just starting your journey, you’ll find a warm welcome here. The club is run by enthusiasts for enthusiasts and their partners. No formality, just a shared love of great cars and camaraderie that comes through them.

Take a look at our events page to see what is on; we have different events and trips booked through the year, so there is sure to be something which takes your fancy. So, go on, give our group a try.

## What We Get Up To

Over the year we enjoy a range of activities together:

- **Regular meetings** — a chance to catch up, swap tips, and talk cars
- **Club runs and drives** — exploring the beautiful roads of South Wales and beyond
- **Shows and events** — attending and displaying at classic car shows in the region
- **Social events** — because it's not just about the cars

## Open to All

You don’t need to own an MG to come along — if you love these cars and want to be around people who do, you’re welcome. Come and see what we’re about and make new friends and contacts in a relaxed and sociable environment.  

We are a club with a wide age group of members, with many having significant experience of owning and working on classic cars. We recognise that more and more people, of all ages, are buying classics and joining clubs like ours to gain knowledge and experience. Within our club, there is always someone who is willing to help and give advice.

As a club, each year we participate in many official classic car shows and events.  In addition, club members organise some great runs outs.  We even plan weekends away. 

If you want to attend everything, or can just make the odd meet and event, that is fine by us. We all have lives outside the club.

Each year the cub supports a different good cause charity. This year the club members have  chosen to support – Matthew Wakelin’s Make a Smile Foundation.
